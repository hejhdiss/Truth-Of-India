Why This Series Exists

Most Indians have never been told the full history. Not because it is secret — it is all publicly documented, in UN resolutions, declassified cables, academic books, and court rulings. But because a systematic effort has been made to keep it buried.

When this series says "educated Indians" — it means every Indian with enough information and critical thinking to see through propaganda. That includes people from Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana — and equally, educated people from every part of India, North or South, who have read the history and not let anyone else do their thinking for them.

South India has historically produced higher literacy rates, stronger press plurality, and more independent political consciousness. But an educated person anywhere in India who has read the actual history will arrive at the same conclusions. This is about information, not geography.

🔴 Economy

The Real Unemployment Numbers

The BJP suppressed the NSSO jobs report for over a year after it showed unemployment had hit a 45-year high of 6.1% in 2017-18 — the worst since 1972. The government denied it, delayed it, and finally released it only after the 2019 election. Among urban educated youth, unemployment exceeded 17%.

Source: NSSO Periodic Labour Force Survey, 2017-18

🔴 Demonetisation

99.3% of Black Money Came Back

The stated purpose of demonetisation — eliminating black money — was documented as a complete failure by the Reserve Bank of India itself. 99.3% of all demonetised currency was returned to the banking system, meaning there was almost no black money destroyed. Meanwhile, 11 million informal sector jobs were wiped out overnight according to CMIE data. 100+ people died in bank queues. The government has never acknowledged the cost.

Source: RBI Annual Report 2018; CMIE employment data

🔴 Wealth Inequality

The 1% Own 40% of India

Under BJP governance, India's wealth inequality has reached levels not seen since the colonial era. According to Oxfam India's 2023 report, the top 1% of Indians now own 40.1% of the country's total wealth. The bottom 50% own just 3%. Billionaire wealth in India grew by 121% in the two years of the COVID-19 pandemic — while 84% of Indian households saw their income decline. This is the economic record behind "Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas."

Source: Oxfam India Inequality Report 2023; World Inequality Database

🔴 Manipur

200+ Dead, PM Silent for Months

The ethnic violence in Manipur that began in May 2023 killed over 200 people and displaced 60,000. The BJP state government imposed an internet blackout that lasted months. Prime Minister Modi did not visit Manipur or make a public statement for over 80 days after the violence began. When he finally spoke, it was only after the sexual assault video went viral internationally. No central government minister was held accountable. The BJP's Manipur government remains in power.

Source: Human Rights Watch; Reuters; Parliament records

🔴 Media Capture

Who Owns What You Watch

The BJP government has presided over the largest consolidation of Indian media in history. Mukesh Ambani's Reliance acquired Network18 — giving a single BJP-aligned billionaire control over CNN-News18, CNBC-TV18, News18 India, and over 20 regional news channels simultaneously. The Adani Group acquired NDTV — once India's most independent news network — in 2022. Two men connected to Modi now control the majority of India's national television news. This is not reported on the channels they own.

Source: Reuters; The Wire; RSF Press Freedom Index 2023

🔴 COVID-19

The Real Death Toll Was Hidden

India's official COVID-19 death toll was approximately 530,000. A peer-reviewed study in Science magazine estimated the actual excess death toll at 3.2 to 4.7 million — making India's true COVID death count among the highest in the world. The discrepancy is explained by systematic undercounting in official state records. Meanwhile, Modi held election rallies and the Kumbh Mela was permitted during the deadly second wave. The government disputed the Science paper and pressured international media not to publish images of mass cremations.

Source: Estimates of excess mortality attributable to COVID-19 in India, Science (2022)

🔴 Pegasus

The Government Spied on Its Own Journalists

The Pegasus Project — a global investigation by 17 media organisations including The Wire — found that Indian journalists, opposition politicians, activists, and even a Supreme Court judge's staff were targeted by Pegasus spyware, which can only be sold to governments. Targets included Rahul Gandhi, Election Commission officials, and journalists investigating the government. The BJP government refused to answer Parliament's questions about Pegasus, claiming "national security." The Supreme Court appointed a technical committee which found evidence of infection on several phones.

Source: The Pegasus Project; Forbidden Stories; Supreme Court technical committee report

🔴 Education

The Degree Mill: 1,500 Fake Universities

India's University Grants Commission (UGC) identified over 1,500 "fake universities" operating across India — charging students tuition for worthless degrees. The majority operate in BJP-governed states with the knowledge of local governments. India simultaneously ranks among the lowest in the world for research output per capita and for employability of graduates. The government responds to this by rewriting NCERT textbooks to remove evolution, not by fixing the examination-mill culture that has destroyed higher education's credibility.

Source: UGC Fake Universities List 2023; Times Higher Education Rankings

🔴 Adani

The Hindenburg Report India Tried to Bury

In January 2023, US short-seller Hindenburg Research published a 106-page report alleging that the Adani Group had engaged in decades of stock manipulation and accounting fraud through a network of offshore shell companies. Adani Group lost $150 billion in market value in days. The government's response: SEBI (India's market regulator) was found by a Supreme Court committee to have potential conflicts of interest in its investigation. Source: Hindenburg Research (2023); US DOJ indictment (2024); Supreme Court SEBI committee

The Law of This Land

Not Per BJP. Not Per RSS.
Per the Constitution of India.

This is not a matter of opinion. It is not a political position. It is the supreme law of the Republic of India — written in blood, sacrifice, and the collective wisdom of people who had just lived through the worst that division can do to a civilisation.

Article 14

"The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India."

Every person. Not every Hindu person. Not every upper-caste person. Not every person who votes a certain way. Every. Person.

Article 15

"The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them."

Religion. Caste. Sex. Place of birth. The Constitution lists them explicitly because the framers knew exactly what kind of discrimination they were writing against. They had seen it. They survived it.

Article 25

"All persons are equally entitled to freedom of conscience and the right freely to profess, practise and propagate religion."

All persons. Muslim. Christian. Hindu. Buddhist. Sikh. Jain. Any faith or no faith. The Constitution does not rank religions. BJP-RSS does.

What the Constitution Says About Women

A woman in India has the exact same constitutional rights as a man. Same right to life. Same right to dignity. Same right to equality before law. Same right to choose her profession, her partner, her faith, her voice. Not as a gift from any party — as a fundamental right that no government can take away. When a Dalit woman is cremated at 2 AM without her family's consent, that is not just a crime. It is a constitutional violation.

What the Constitution Says About Minorities

Articles 29 and 30 guarantee every minority community — religious or linguistic — the right to conserve its culture and establish educational institutions. The Constitution does not make minorities guests in their own country. It makes them equal citizens with specific protections. When BJP calls Muslims "infiltrators" and RSS literature calls them foreigners, they are not being nationalist. They are contradicting the Constitution they swore an oath to uphold.

What the Constitution Says About Dalits

Article 17 abolishes untouchability — completely, permanently, without exception. Not "reduce it." Not "manage it." Abolish. Every time a Dalit child is beaten for touching a water pot, every time a Dalit woman is cremated without dignity, every time a Dalit family's home is bulldozed — the Constitution is being violated. Dr. Ambedkar wrote Article 17 knowing exactly what it needed to protect against. That fight is not over.

These are documented statistical realities, not insults to anyone.

Because the conclusions follow from the facts. Geography does not determine understanding. Information does.

The RSS brainwash pipeline has been most effective in states where it has had the deepest penetration and the least competition from independent press. That is a structural observation — not an ethnic one. This series is written for every Indian who wants to think for themselves. North, South, East, West.
