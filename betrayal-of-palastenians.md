# When Refugees Became Occupiers: The Betrayal of Palestine

*A perspective from South India — where we know colonialism when we see it.*

---

## They Were Given Shelter. Then They Took the House.

There is an old saying in Kerala: *"Don't give shelter to a snake."* It is a harsh saying. But history sometimes earns harsh sayings.

In the early 20th century, Palestine was home to Arab Muslims, Arab Christians, and a Jewish minority — all living on the same land. When Jewish refugees began fleeing persecution in Europe, it was the Palestinian people — among others — who shared that land with them. They were not turned away. They were not driven into the sea. They were neighbors.

What happened next is one of the most documented betrayals in modern history.

---

## The Nakba: The Catastrophe They Don't Want You to Know

In 1948, the State of Israel was declared. In that same year and the years surrounding it, over **750,000 Palestinians** were expelled or fled from their homes in what Palestinians call the **Nakba** — Arabic for "The Catastrophe."

This was not a natural disaster. It was a deliberate campaign of displacement.

Villages were demolished. Over **500 Palestinian villages** were destroyed or depopulated. Families who had lived on the same land for generations were forced into refugee camps in Gaza, the West Bank, Jordan, Lebanon, and Syria — where many of their descendants still live today, over 75 years later.

The United Nations passed **Resolution 194** in 1948, affirming the right of Palestinian refugees to return to their homes. Israel has never complied. That resolution still exists. That right is still denied.

---

## Refugees Who Created Refugees

This is the central historical irony — and it is not a small one.

The founders of the Israeli state were, in significant part, people who had survived or fled persecution in Europe. The world recognized their suffering. The world supported their need for safety.

But the cost of that safety was paid by the Palestinians — people who had nothing to do with European antisemitism, nothing to do with the Holocaust, nothing to do with the persecution of Jews in Russia or Germany or Poland.

The Palestinians paid the price for Europe's crimes.

A people who knew what it meant to be refugees — to be stateless, to be displaced, to have the world ignore their suffering — built a state by making another people stateless, displaced, and ignored.

---

## Occupation: The Word the West Tries to Avoid

Since 1967, Israel has militarily occupied the West Bank and Gaza Strip. The **United Nations**, the **International Court of Justice**, and virtually every international legal body considers this occupation illegal under international law.

In the occupied West Bank, Israeli settlements — built on Palestinian land — continue to expand to this day, in direct violation of international law. Palestinians living there require permits to move, to build, to farm their own land. Their water is controlled. Their movement is restricted. Their homes are demolished.

In Gaza, over 2 million people live in what has been called — by human rights organizations including the UN — an **open-air prison**. For years, Israel has controlled what food, medicine, and goods can enter.

This is not self-defense. This is a system of control built on land that was taken.

---

## What History Records

History is not always comfortable. But it records clearly:

- Palestinian Arabs lived on this land for centuries before the mass migration of the 20th century.
- They did not expel the Jewish people who came. They shared the land.
- They were then expelled themselves — through military force, through destruction of villages, through laws that stripped them of citizenship and property rights.
- Their descendants have been living as refugees and under occupation ever since.
- Every attempt by international bodies to enforce their rights has been blocked, vetoed, or ignored — primarily by the United States.

This is not anti-Jewish to say. Many Jewish historians, Israeli historians, and human rights activists say the same thing. Scholars like **Ilan Pappé**, **Benny Morris**, and **Neve Gordon** — Israelis themselves — have documented this history in detail.

---

## Why This Matters in India

From Kerala, from South India, from a land that knows what British colonialism looked like — we should be able to recognize a colonial structure when we see one.

The argument being fed to some Indians — that supporting Israel is about fighting terrorism, or about Hindu-Muslim tension, or about being "modern" and "anti-Islamist" — is a political manipulation. It takes the suffering of Palestinians and reframes it as something else entirely.

The question is simple: Is it right for one people to take another people's land, demolish their villages, confine their descendants to refugee camps for 75 years, and continue building settlements on what remains?

That question has nothing to do with religion. It has everything to do with justice.

## Now Iran: Israel Started This War Too. The Record Is Clear.

Some people ask: "But didn't Iran attack Israel first?" Let's look at the actual timeline — not the one fed by Western media, but what actually happened, in order.

**April 1, 2024** — Israel bombed the Iranian consulate in Damascus, Syria. A consulate is legally sovereign territory under international law. Israel killed senior Iranian military officials inside it. This was an act of war by any legal definition.

**April 13, 2024** — Iran responded with missile and drone strikes on Israel. This was *retaliation*, not initiation.

**July 31, 2024** — Israel assassinated Hamas political leader Ismail Haniyeh *inside Tehran* — the capital of Iran. Not on a battlefield. Not in Gaza. Inside Iran's capital city.

**June 13, 2025** — Israel launched a full surprise attack on Iranian nuclear and military facilities. *While diplomatic negotiations between the US and Iran were still ongoing.* Iran had been at the negotiating table. Israel struck anyway — mid-diplomacy.

**February 28, 2026** — The US and Israel launched a massive coordinated assault on Iran, codenamed "Operation Roaring Lion" by Israel and "Operation Epic Fury" by the United States. Trump announced the stated goal openly: regime change. Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was assassinated. Strikes hit Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, and Kermanshah. This came just two days after nuclear negotiations in Geneva had collapsed.

This is the sequence. Israel struck first. Iran responded. Israel struck again. And each time, the Western media framing described Iran as the aggressor.

## They Bombed a Girls' School. Children Aged 6 to 11.

This is not a metaphor. This is not propaganda. This is what happened, reported by Al Jazeera, CNN, and Iran's Health Ministry simultaneously.

On February 28, 2026 — the first day of the US-Israeli assault — a strike hit the **Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' elementary school** in Minab, in southern Iran's Hormozgan province. The children inside were between 6 and 11 years old.

The death toll from that single strike rose to **165 children**. Ninety-six more were injured. Footage circulated of a man clutching the remains of a child he said was six or seven years old, accusing the US and Israel of war crimes.

The Israeli military said it was "not aware" of strikes in the area. The US military said it was "looking into reports."

One hundred and sixty-five children. In a school. On the first day.

By March 5, the World Health Organization had confirmed **13 separate attacks on Iranian health infrastructure** by the US and Israel. The total Iranian death toll from US-Israeli strikes rose to over **1,300**, according to Iran's Tasnim news agency.

Iran's strikes on Israel and surrounding countries also killed and injured civilians. A woman in Tel Aviv was killed on the first day. A strike on Beit Shemesh killed nine people. Iranian drones struck civilian infrastructure across the Gulf region including airports and residential buildings. Those deaths are real too.

But there is a crucial difference: **Iran was responding to an attack launched against it. The US and Israel chose to launch a surprise assault — planned months in advance — while peace negotiations were on the table.**

An Israeli defense official confirmed that the launch date had been decided weeks in advance and planned in full coordination with Washington.

---

## False Justifications, Familiar Playbook

Trump announced the strikes at 2:30 AM in an 8-minute video, citing Iran's "menacing activities" — the Iran hostage crisis of 1979, support for Hamas and Hezbollah, killings of protesters, and an alleged nuclear weapons program.

Iran has maintained consistently — and the IAEA had confirmed repeatedly — that its nuclear program was civilian in nature. Iran entered indirect nuclear negotiations specifically, in its own words, *to demonstrate its legitimacy and the illegitimacy of any pretext* used to justify an attack.

The playbook is not new. "Pre-emptive self-defense." Alleged weapons programs. Regime change framed as liberation. This is the same language used to justify the invasion of Iraq in 2003 — a war based on fabricated intelligence about weapons of mass destruction that killed hundreds of thousands of civilians and destabilized an entire region for decades. History is repeating itself, in the same region, with the same actors.

---

## The Pattern Does Not Change

Bomb the consulate. Assassinate leaders on foreign soil. Strike while diplomacy is happening. Kill 165 children in a school on day one. Then tell the world you are the victim.

This is not a pattern of self-defense. This is a pattern of a state — and now a superpower backing it — that believes it operates above international law. Because for decades, it has been allowed to.

Russia requested an emergency IAEA Board session the same day. The EU called for maximum restraint and protection of civilians. The UN called on all parties to respect international law. Most of the world condemned the strikes.

No binding resolution came from the Security Council — because the United States sits on it with veto power, and it was one of the two countries doing the bombing.

## Kerala Stands With Palestine — And Now With Iran

This is not a new position for Kerala. It is a very old one.

Kerala has had trade, cultural, and human ties with the Arab world for over a thousand years — long before European colonizers drew the maps we use today. The Malabar coast welcomed Arab traders, scholars, and settlers. The oldest mosque in India — the **Cheraman Juma Masjid** in Kodungallur, Malappuram district — was built in 629 CE, during the lifetime of the Prophet Muhammad himself. Arab and Keralite families intermarried, traded, and built a shared civilization across the Arabian Sea.

When Palestinians suffer, it is not an abstract geopolitical event for Keralites. Many of our families have worked in the Gulf for generations. We have sat in the homes of Arab families. We have broken bread with Palestinians, Iranians, Yemenis, Jordanians. We know these are human beings — not statistics, not "collateral damage."

---

## Kerala's Political Voice Has Been Clear

The Kerala Legislative Assembly has passed **resolutions condemning Israeli military actions** in Gaza — one of the few state assemblies in India to do so formally. Massive public protests have taken place across the state, in Thiruvananthapuram, Kozhikode, Malappuram, and Thrissur. Civil society organizations, student unions, trade unions, and religious groups across communities — Muslim, Christian, and Hindu — have stood together in solidarity.

This is not because Keralites are told what to think. It is because Keralites have historically had the education, the political consciousness, and the direct human connections to look at what is happening and call it what it is.

---

## We Recognize Colonialism. We Lived It.

Kerala was colonized. The Portuguese came to our coast in 1498 — and with them came violence, forced conversions, the destruction of mosques and temples, and the disruption of our ancient trade networks. The Dutch came. The British came. Each time, people who arrived claiming civilization brought exploitation.

We know what it looks like when a powerful outside force arrives, takes land, rewrites the rules to serve itself, and calls any resistance "terrorism." We have seen that story. We lived inside it.

What Israel is doing in Palestine — and what the US and Israel together have now done to Iran — fits that pattern precisely. Arrive with power. Take what you want. Kill those who resist. Use the language of self-defense to justify the violence of aggression.

As Keralites, as South Indians, as people with our own memory of occupation — we do not need Western media to tell us which side of this line to stand on.

We stand with the people whose homes were taken. We stand with the children in the school in Minab. We stand with the families in Gaza who have nowhere left to go. We stand with the truth, even when powerful governments try to bury it.

---



Kerala has its own memory of being colonized, exploited, and having its resources extracted by people who came from elsewhere and decided they had the right to stay and rule. We know what that looks like. We know what it produces — generations of anger, of lost identity, of fighting just to exist.

What is happening in Palestine is not complicated once you look at it clearly. A people was displaced. They have been asking to go home for 75 years. The world keeps saying "soon" — and the settlements keep expanding.

History will record this. It always does.
