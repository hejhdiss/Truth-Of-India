# The Children Left Behind: How BJP and RSS Policies Have Failed India's Innocents

*A Critical Investigative Report | Sources: UNICEF, Save the Children, National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR), Human Rights Watch, Child Rights and You (CRY), Reuters, The Wire, Al Jazeera, BBC, Article 14, Scroll.in, ASER Centre (Annual Status of Education Report), UNESCO, Lancet, IndiaSpend*

## Introduction

A nation is judged not by the wealth of its richest, but by the safety of its most helpless. Children cannot vote. They cannot protest. They cannot call press conferences. They depend entirely on the adults and institutions around them to protect their bodies, fill their stomachs, educate their minds, and secure their futures.

In India under BJP and RSS governance since 2014, the documented evidence shows a systematic failure of this duty — across education, nutrition, child labour, sexual violence, and the targeting of children from minority and marginalised communities. This is not a failure of resources alone. India is the fifth-largest economy in the world. It is a failure of priorities — of a government that has spent more money on a new Parliament building, a new Prime Minister's residence, and electoral bond collections than on the basic needs of 472 million children.

Every number in this article comes from India's own government data, from UNICEF, from peer-reviewed public health research, or from India's own courts and child rights commissions.

## Part 1: Child Malnutrition — A National Emergency Ignored

### India's Hunger Shame

India is home to the largest number of malnourished children on earth. This is not a historical legacy that has been slowly improving. Under BJP governance, the data shows stagnation and, in key indicators, reversal.

According to the **National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)**, conducted in 2019–2021 and published during the BJP government's tenure:

- **35.5% of Indian children under five are stunted** — meaning permanently shortened in height and cognitive development due to chronic malnutrition
- **19.3% are wasted** — dangerously thin, at acute risk of death
- **32.1% are underweight**

India accounts for approximately **one-third of all stunted children in the world**. This is not a statistic from the 1980s. This is a measurement taken in 2019–2021, more than five years into BJP governance, after years of "Shining India" and "Vishwaguru" narrative.

The **Global Hunger Index (GHI) 2023** ranked India **111th out of 125 countries** — below neighbours Nepal (69th), Bangladesh (81st), Myanmar (71st), and Pakistan (102nd). The BJP government's response to this ranking was not to address hunger, but to call the index "biased" and "an attempt to taint India's image."

UNICEF India's 2023 report found that **the COVID-19 period erased years of modest gains in child nutrition**, and that recovery under the current government's programmes has been inadequate. The Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) scheme — India's main nutrition programme for children — has been chronically underfunded. A 2021 parliamentary committee report found that thousands of anganwadi centres (community nutrition posts) were operating without proper buildings, water, or electricity.

### Mid-Day Meal Deaths: Children Killed by Indifference

The Mid-Day Meal scheme — a programme that provides free cooked lunches to government school children — is one of India's most consequential social programmes. It keeps children in school and reduces hunger. It has also, repeatedly, become a site of tragedy due to criminal negligence.

In July 2013, in Saran district, Bihar — not yet a BJP state — 23 children died after eating a contaminated mid-day meal. It remains India's worst food safety disaster involving children in schools. But it is not an isolated case. The NCRB and media investigations documented dozens of mass food poisoning incidents in school meals across BJP-governed states between 2014 and 2024, with hundreds of children hospitalised every year. In almost every case, the investigation found the same causes: pesticide-contaminated ingredients, unhygienic cooking conditions, underfunded contractors cutting costs, and zero accountability for the officials who approved contracts.

## Part 2: Education — What BJP is Teaching India's Children

### The NCERT Textbook Purge

The most direct window into what the RSS-BJP government intends for the next generation is the content of what children are taught. Between 2022 and 2024, the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) — the body that writes India's school textbooks — conducted a sweeping revision of curricula under BJP-appointed leadership. The results, documented by journalists, academics, and opposition politicians, reveal a systematic ideological rewriting of children's education.

Removed or substantially reduced from textbooks:
- **Charles Darwin's theory of evolution** — removed from Class 10 science textbooks
- **The Emergency period (1975–77)** — reduced or removed from Class 12 political science
- **Chapters on the Mughal Empire** — reduced or removed from history texts
- **The Gujarat riots of 2002** — removed from political science texts
- **The demolition of the Babri Masjid** — context and consequences reduced
- **Mahatma Gandhi's assassination** — context naming the RSS's ideological connection to the assassin was removed
- **Mentions of caste discrimination** — reduced across multiple subjects

Added or expanded:
- Content presenting RSS ideology and Hindutva figures positively
- Chapters emphasising Hindu civilisational history in ways that exclude Muslim and other contributions
- "Yoga and wellness" content developed in line with RSS cultural priorities

The changes were condemned by the **Indian History Congress**, by hundreds of academic historians in open letters, by UNESCO officials who expressed concern about India's commitment to secular education, and by opposition parties. The government's response was that the changes were "rationalisation" and "removing outdated content."

What does it mean to remove evolution from science education? It means India's children — who will need to compete in biotechnology, medicine, and research against children from countries with rigorous science curricula — will reach university with a foundational gap in biological literacy. This is not a religious question. It is a question of whether India is equipping its children to survive in the twenty-first century.

### ASER Data: Half of India's Children Cannot Read

The Annual Status of Education Report (ASER), published by the NGO Pratham, is the most comprehensive independent assessment of learning outcomes in Indian schools. Its findings, consistent across every year of BJP governance, are devastating:

- **ASER 2022**: Only **42.8% of Class 5 students** could read a Class 2-level text. More than half of ten-year-olds in Indian government schools cannot read a basic paragraph.
- Basic arithmetic performance has similarly stagnated, with fewer than half of Class 5 students able to do simple division.

India has near-universal school enrolment. The problem is not whether children are in school. The problem is whether they are learning anything when they get there. The BJP government's response has been to point to infrastructure — new school buildings, toilets, digital devices — while the learning crisis deepens. Brick and mortar does not teach a child to read. Teachers do. And India's government school system is chronically understaffed, with teachers often diverted to election duty, data entry, and census work rather than teaching.

### The RSS Shakha Pipeline: Indoctrination as Education

Parallel to formal schooling, the RSS operates a network of tens of thousands of **shakhas** — daily physical and ideological training sessions for boys and young men. The RSS claims over 50,000 active shakhas across India.

Human Rights Watch and academic researchers have documented the content of shakha instruction: physical drills combined with ideological lessons presenting Muslims and Christians as threats to Hindu civilisation, presenting India as essentially a Hindu nation in which minorities are guests rather than equals, and cultivating a martial masculine identity defined in opposition to the religious other.

This is not education. It is the systematic manufacture of communal hostility in children, running parallel to and increasingly shaping formal school curricula through the RSS's control over NCERT and state education boards in BJP-governed states.

## Part 3: Child Sexual Abuse — The POCSO Crisis

### The Scale of the Crime

The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act was passed in 2012 and is among India's strongest child protection laws. The NCRB data on POCSO cases reveals a picture of widespread abuse and a justice system failing to address it:

- In 2022, **51,863 cases were registered under POCSO** — a rate of more than 140 cases per day
- The conviction rate in POCSO cases remains extremely low — in many states below 30%
- Pendency of POCSO cases in courts across India runs into hundreds of thousands of cases, with children waiting years for justice
- Cases involving accused with political connections — particularly in BJP-governed states — show documented patterns of investigation delays, witness intimidation, and bail granted to accused over victim objections

The Kathua case: In January 2018, an eight-year-old Bakarwal Muslim girl named Asifa Bano was abducted, held in a Hindu temple in Kathua, Jammu, gang-raped over multiple days, and murdered. When police filed a charge sheet against the accused, BJP ministers in the Jammu & Kashmir government **participated in a rally defending the accused** — not the child. The ministers held Indian flags. Lawyers associated with the local bar attempted to block the filing of the charge sheet in court.

It took nationwide outrage, international media coverage, and Supreme Court monitoring before the case moved forward. Six men were convicted in 2019. The child was eight years old.

### Children in Detention

Under the BJP government's Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) and National Register of Citizens (NRC) processes, thousands of people in Assam and other states were placed in detention centres on citizenship grounds. Among those affected: **children**, including children born in India to parents deemed "foreigners" by the NRC process.

Human Rights Watch documented cases of children separated from parents detained in NRC camps, and of families living under the threat that their children could be rendered stateless. UNICEF expressed concern. The Assam NRC process — conducted under BJP governance in Assam — produced a list of 1.9 million people declared non-citizens, many of them Hindus and Bengalis who had lived in India their entire lives. The resulting legal, social, and psychological trauma fell disproportionately on the most vulnerable — including children too young to understand why their families were being told they did not belong in the country of their birth.

## Part 4: Child Labour — The Economy's Dirty Secret

### 10 Million Children Working Instead of Learning

India officially prohibits child labour under the Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Amendment Act 2016. The reality, documented by the International Labour Organisation and India's own census data, is that India has one of the world's largest child labour populations.

Census 2011 data — the most recent comprehensive count — recorded approximately **10.1 million child workers** aged 5–14 in India. Economists and labour rights researchers estimate the actual number is significantly higher, as much child labour occurs in family enterprises and agricultural settings where it is systematically undercounted.

Under BJP governance since 2014, enforcement of child labour laws has weakened alongside the general erosion of labour inspection systems. The 2016 amendment to the child labour law — passed by the BJP government — actually **expanded the definition of "family enterprise"** in which children are permitted to work after school hours, a provision that labour rights activists and UNICEF criticised as creating a legal loophole that enables child labour in agricultural and small business settings to continue under the guise of "family work."

Children in India's carpet belt (UP), brick kilns (Bihar, UP, Rajasthan), agricultural fields (multiple states), and urban domestic service sector work in conditions of debt bondage and near-total legal invisibility. They do not attend school. They will not access the digital economy. They will not double anyone's income. They have been written out of the future.

## Part 5: Minority Children — Targeted by Policy and Violence

### Muslim Children and Educational Exclusion

Under BJP governance, multiple policies have converged to systematically disadvantage Muslim children's access to quality education:

- The **Sachar Committee Report** (2006) had documented that Muslim children had the lowest educational attainment of any major community in India. BJP governments have systematically defunded the Maulana Azad National Fellowship, the primary scholarship programme for minority students, with it being **discontinued in 2022**
- Madrasas — which serve as the primary educational institution for millions of Muslim children in poorer states — have been subjected to increasingly hostile regulation, with multiple BJP states announcing madrasa surveys, demanding registration, and in some cases ordering closure
- Muslim girls wearing hijabs were **barred from entering classrooms** in Karnataka in 2022, forcing students to choose between their religious identity and their education. The Karnataka High Court upheld the ban; the Supreme Court bench was split. Girls who refused to remove their hijab missed exams and lost academic years

These are children. They have done nothing wrong. They were told by the state that they must look a certain way to be permitted to learn.

### Dalit Children: Caste Violence in Schools

India's schools are not caste-neutral environments. Human Rights Watch's landmark report *"Broken People"* and subsequent investigations by Article 14 and Scroll document systematic caste-based discrimination against Dalit children in government schools across India:

- Dalit children made to sit separately from upper-caste peers
- Dalit children assigned cleaning duties while upper-caste children are not
- Mid-day meals served to Dalit children separately, or Dalit children required to bring their own plates while upper-caste children use shared utensils
- Teachers from upper-caste backgrounds refusing to touch the work of Dalit students

In September 2020 — the same month as Hathras — a nine-year-old Dalit boy in Jalore, Rajasthan, **died after his upper-caste teacher beat him for touching a water pot reserved for upper-caste people.** The child's name was Indra Kumar Meghwal. He had only wanted a drink of water.

Under BJP governance, caste atrocity cases have increased — the NCRB data shows crimes against Scheduled Castes rising year on year — while conviction rates remain extremely low. The state governments that have been slowest to prosecute caste crimes are predominantly BJP-governed.

## Conclusion: A Country That Builds Statues While Children Starve

India built the world's tallest statue — the Statue of Unity, at a cost of ₹2,989 crore — while anganwadi workers who feed malnourished children earned less than ₹5,000 per month. India built a new Parliament building worth ₹971 crore while ASER data showed that half of Class 5 children could not read. India removed evolution from Class 10 science textbooks while competing with China and the United States for technology leadership.

The children do not have a lobby. They do not have electoral bonds. They do not have a minister in cabinet. They have only the promise — the constitutional promise, the civilisational promise — that the adults in charge of this country will put their welfare first.

That promise has been broken. Thirty farmers' children will today grow up without a father because he died by suicide over a debt he could never repay. A girl in Karnataka missed her exams because she would not remove her hijab. A nine-year-old boy died in Rajasthan because he touched a water pot. The children of Manipur grew up watching their parents flee with nothing.

**This is what the data shows. This is what the BJP has presided over. Every number is checkable. Every case is documented. Check them.**
