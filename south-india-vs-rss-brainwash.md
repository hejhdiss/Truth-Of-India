## The RSS Brainwash Pipeline: How It Works

RSS has been running one of the most sophisticated ideological manufacturing operations in the world for nearly a century. Here is how the pipeline works, explained simply:

**Step 1 — Start young.** RSS shakhas recruit boys from childhood. Before a young man can think critically, he has already been taught who the heroes are, who the enemies are, and what "real India" looks like. Critical thinking is not on the curriculum. Loyalty is.

**Step 2 — Control the textbooks.** After BJP came to power at the centre, NCERT textbooks in Hindi-medium schools were systematically revised. References to the Mughal contribution to Indian culture: reduced. Mentions of Gandhi's assassin's ideological roots: removed. The actual history of the RSS during the independence movement: quietly edited. Millions of children in Hindi-belt states are now learning a version of history that was designed in an RSS office.

**Step 3 — Control the television.** Republic TV. Zee News. News18 Hindi. These are not news channels in any meaningful sense. They are RSS-adjacent propaganda machines that run 18 hours a day telling their audience who to fear, who to hate, and who to cheer for. In Kerala, we have *Mathrubhumi*, *Manorama*, *MediaOne*, *Asianet* — competing outlets with different political lines. Competition produces something closer to reality. Monopoly produces something closer to a cult.

**Step 4 — Give them an enemy.** Every successful brainwash needs an enemy. Muslims. Pakistan. "Urban Naxals." "Tukde tukde gang." Once you have given someone an enemy, you can get them to support almost anything — including a foreign government that is bombing children — as long as you frame it as fighting the same enemy.

By the end of this pipeline, a person can watch footage of a Palestinian family losing their home and think: *"Good. That's what happens to Muslims."*

That is not stupidity in the clinical sense. That is **manufactured stupidity** — which is actually harder to fix, because the person doesn't know it happened to them.

## Kerala Escaped the Pipeline. Here Is Why.

Kerala's literacy rate is over **96%** — the highest in India. But literacy alone is not the full answer. Plenty of literate people fall for propaganda.

What Kerala has that most of North India does not:

**A Left political tradition that taught class consciousness.** For decades, Kerala's political landscape was shaped by communist and socialist movements that explicitly taught workers to question power, question media, question the state. You cannot run an RSS shakha in a village where the local union has been teaching people to think critically since 1957.

**Genuine press plurality.** Kerala has more newspapers per capita than almost any state in India. Those newspapers disagree with each other loudly and publicly. When media competes, truth has a better chance of surviving.

**Direct connection to the Arab world.** Millions of Keralites have worked in the Gulf. Their families have sat in Arab homes, eaten Arab food, watched Arab children grow up. Palestine is not an abstract news story in Kerala. It is the home of people our families know. You cannot be brainwashed into seeing those people as subhuman when you have shared meals with them.

**A tradition of reading.** Kerala has the highest number of public libraries per capita in India. Reading — actual books, actual history — is a cultural norm here in a way it simply is not in states where RSS has replaced the library with the shakha.

## The Origin Story: A Land Promised by People Who Didn't Own It

Before we talk about Modi, let's go back to where this whole thing started — because the founding of Israel is itself a masterclass in colonial arrogance.

In 1917, during World War I, the British Empire was exhausted and heavily in debt. Beginning in 1916, the British hoped that in exchange for their support of Zionism, Jewish financiers would help fund the growing expenses of the First World War. So British Foreign Secretary Arthur Balfour wrote a letter — addressed directly to **Lord Walter Rothschild**, the most prominent Jewish financier in Britain — promising British support for a Jewish homeland in Palestine.

The Balfour Declaration was a public pledge by Britain declaring its aim to establish "a national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine — made during World War I and later included in the terms of the British Mandate.

Here is the problem stated plainly by Palestinian-American scholar Edward Said, as quoted by Al Jazeera: it was "made by a European power about a non-European territory in a flat disregard of both the presence and wishes of the native majority resident in that territory." In other words: Britain promised a land it did not own, to people who did not live there, without asking the people who actually lived there — who made up over 90% of the population at the time.

The Balfour Declaration of 1917 wasn't a sudden act of humanitarianism; it was a debt settlement disguised as diplomacy — a private agreement between a creditor and a debtor. Palestine was the payment. The Palestinians were not consulted. They were not even informed.

## "God Promised It to Us 3,000 Years Ago" 😐

And now, the funniest and saddest argument currently circulating on social media in defense of Israeli territorial claims:

*"God promised this land to the Jewish people 3,000 years ago."*

Let's think about this for a moment — with our full brains intact, unlike some.

If "God promised it to us 3,000 years ago" is a valid legal basis for territorial ownership, then:

- Every kingdom that ever existed in history can reclaim every piece of land mentioned in any religious text
- The Cholas can reclaim Southeast Asia
- The Mughals can reclaim Central Asia
- Every displaced people in history has a claim to somewhere based on some scripture written by someone

The international community does not recognize divine real estate contracts as legally binding. The United Nations does not have a department for processing God's promises. The International Court of Justice operates on international law, not ancient scripture.

But more importantly — even if we *were* to take the religious argument seriously — the Bible itself records that many different peoples lived in and around that land across thousands of years. "Promised land" theology was contested even within ancient Jewish tradition.

The actual legal basis for Israel's existence is the 1948 UN partition plan. That plan itself was deeply contested by the Arab world — and it allocated land that was overwhelmingly populated by Palestinians. The Palestinians did not accept being partitioned out of their own homeland. That is not terrorism. That is a human response to dispossession.

The "3,000 years" argument is what happens when the RSS pipeline has successfully replaced your brain with a WhatsApp forward.

## Indira Gandhi Had a Spine. What Happened to Ours?

This section is for every Indian who uses the word "sovereignty" and then watches their Prime Minister quietly nod while America and Israel decide the fate of the Middle East.

In December 1971, during the Bangladesh Liberation War, US President Richard Nixon — who privately called Indians "slippery, treacherous people" and referred to Indira Gandhi in terms unfit to print — ordered the US Seventh Fleet's Task Force 74, led by the nuclear-powered aircraft carrier USS Enterprise, to proceed towards the Bay of Bengal to intimidate India into stopping its military advance.

Gandhi dismissed the threat, reportedly daring Nixon to "send the eighth fleet." She had already signed a mutual defense treaty with the Soviet Union. The Soviets dispatched a nuclear-armed fleet that tracked and encircled the American task force in the Indian Ocean. The United States Seventh Fleet eventually turned away.

India finished liberating Bangladesh. 93,000 Pakistani soldiers surrendered — the largest military surrender since World War II. And Nixon, who had called her a "bitch" in the Oval Office, had to watch from Washington as Indira Gandhi proved that India was not America's client state.

**That was a Prime Minister with a spinal cord.**

Now look at what we have.

Narendra Modi — whose government has abstained or voted against UN resolutions condemning Israeli military operations in Gaza. Modi — who flew to Israel and was photographed warmly embracing Netanyahu while Gaza was being bombed. Modi — who has not once, in ten years of leading India, challenged an American foreign policy decision in any meaningful way. Modi — whose government's silence on the US-Israeli strikes on Iran in February 2026 was deafening, when India has millions of citizens living across the Gulf region affected by that war.

Where is the Indo-Soviet Treaty equivalent? Where is the independent foreign policy? Where is the "strategic autonomy" that every BJP spokesperson loves to claim while doing exactly what Washington signals?

Indira Gandhi stood up to America when America sent a nuclear carrier to intimidate her. Modi sends congratulatory messages.
