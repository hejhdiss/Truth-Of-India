## Let Us Start With a Simple Metaphor

Imagine you own a house. It is your house — built by your grandparents, defended by your parents, inherited by you after a long and difficult struggle. You have your own rules inside that house. You decide who comes in, what you eat, who you trade with, what flag flies over your roof.

Now imagine your neighbor — a large, powerful, wealthy neighbor — starts telling you:

*"You can only buy groceries for 30 days at a time. I need to approve it."*

*"Don't talk to that other neighbor. I don't like him."*

*"Send some of your workers to help me build my house. I'll pay them less than I'd pay my own people."*

*"Also, don't say anything publicly when I bomb the family three streets over. Just smile and wave."*

And you — the owner of your own house, the inheritor of a civilization 5,000 years old — say: *okay.*

That neighbor is America. The house is India. The person saying *okay* is Narendra Modi.

This is not a metaphor anymore. This is the documented foreign policy record of the last ten years.

## The Puppet Test: Let Us Apply It Honestly

A puppet is not someone who cooperates with powerful countries. Every country cooperates with powerful countries — that is normal diplomacy.

A puppet is someone who **cannot say no.** Someone whose decisions consistently favor the puppeteer over their own country's citizens, interests, and dignity. Someone who performs sovereignty loudly — big speeches, big slogans, *Vishwaguru*, *56-inch chest* — while quietly doing exactly what they are told.

Let us apply the test:

**Test 1 — Energy policy:**
During the US-Israel war on Iran in 2026, the Strait of Hormuz was disrupted — accounting for 40% of India's oil imports. India desperately needed Russian oil. What did Modi's government do? It went to Donald Trump and *asked for permission* to buy Russian oil. Trump issued a 30-day waiver. India said thank you.

Indira Gandhi told Nixon to take his aircraft carrier out of the Indian Ocean. Modi asked Trump for a 30-day grocery permit.

**✅ Puppet confirmed. No independent leader asks permission to buy their own country's groceries.**

**Test 2 — Palestine:**
India has historically supported Palestinian rights at the UN since the time of Nehru. Under Modi, India's voting record quietly shifted — abstaining on resolutions that previous governments supported clearly. While Gaza was being bombed and over 45,000 Palestinians killed, Modi visited Israel, embraced Netanyahu, and said not one word about Palestinian civilian deaths.

Can you find a single statement from Modi directly criticizing Israeli military actions? One. Just one.

**✅ Puppet confirmed. Silence when your master speaks is not diplomacy. It is obedience.**

**Test 3 — International law and independent voice:**
When America says "don't do this," does India do it anyway if it is in India's interest? Vajpayee did — he tested nuclear weapons in 1998 against enormous American pressure and absorbed the sanctions. Indira Gandhi signed a treaty with the Soviets when America tried to intimidate her. Nehru co-founded the Non-Aligned Movement specifically to resist superpower pressure.

Modi? Name one time. One single time he said no to Washington on a matter of principle and meant it.

**✅ Puppet confirmed. You cannot name one. Because there isn't one.**

Three tests. Three times confirmed. The verdict is in.

## Netanyahu's Personal Request — And Modi Delivered

This is not speculation. This is documented by the Boston Review, NPR, NBC News, The Diplomat, Al Jazeera, and the Washington Post simultaneously.

In December 2023 — while Gaza was already being bombed, while Palestinian civilians were already dying in their thousands — Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu made a **personal request** to his "friend and political ally" Narendra Modi: *expedite the supply of Indian construction workers to Israel.*

Before October 7, 2023, Palestinians from the West Bank and Gaza made up the majority of workers in Israel's construction and agriculture sectors. After October 7, Israel terminated over **200,000 work permits** granted to Palestinian workers. In one move, Israel removed the Palestinian workforce that had been building Israeli infrastructure for decades — the same Palestinians whose land Israel was simultaneously bombing.

And who filled the gap? Who replaced the Palestinian workers?

Indians. Specifically, poor Indians from Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and Haryana — India's most economically desperate states, where unemployment for young graduates exceeds 42%.

Within weeks of Netanyahu's personal request to Modi, the state governments of Haryana and Uttar Pradesh — both BJP-governed — advertised 10,000 construction jobs each in Israel. Government recruitment centers were set up. Israeli recruitment teams flew to Lucknow. Long lines of desperate young Indian men — carpenters, masons, plasterers, plumbers — queued for days in the cold for a chance to earn ₹1,30,000 per month in Israel, five or six times what they could earn at home.

By 2024, **16,000 Indian workers had replaced Palestinian workers** in Israel's construction sector. Israel's target: **100,000 Indian workers** total.

## Let Us Be Clear About What This Is

Poor Indians — driven to queue in the cold by unemployment that Modi's government has failed to address — are being sent to replace Palestinians who were expelled from their jobs so that Israel could wage war on them more effectively.

Indian workers, by filling those jobs, are directly enabling the Israeli economy to sustain its military operations in Gaza. Indian tax money goes to a government that facilitates this. Indian state governments — BJP-governed — run the recruitment drives.

The Boston Review called India's recruitment drives a structure that "resembles British indenture." The same structure that sent Indian workers as indentured laborers to Fiji, Mauritius, Trinidad, and South Africa under British colonial rule — where they worked for wages so low they could barely survive, in conditions they had no power to contest.

India fought for independence from that system. Now its own government is recreating it — for the benefit of a foreign state that is simultaneously bombing the people those Indian workers are replacing.

And the workers themselves? They know there are bombs. "We've heard there are lots of bombs and missiles being thrown around," one applicant told NPR. He applied anyway. Because in Modi's India, a war zone pays better than home.

That is not a foreign policy achievement. That is a humanitarian failure dressed up as bilateral cooperation.

## "Don't Get Involved in International Affairs" — Said America. India Nodded.

There is a well-documented pattern in how the Modi government responds to American foreign policy signals.

When America wanted India to distance itself from Russia after Ukraine: India reduced Russian oil purchases and signed a trade deal committing to buy American energy instead.

When America wanted India to stay quiet on Palestine: India went quiet on Palestine.

When Netanyahu personally asked Modi for workers: Modi delivered the workers.

When America launched strikes on Iran — a country with which India has deep historical, civilizational, and strategic ties, including the Chabahar port project that India spent years and billions developing — Modi's government issued careful, balanced statements. Not condemnation. Not solidarity with the Iranian civilians being bombed. Careful. Balanced. Inoffensive to Washington.

There is a phrase in Malayalam: *"കൂട്ടിൽ കിടക്കുന്ന കിളി"* — the bird that sits quietly in its cage.

India under Modi is a caged bird that has learned to call its cage a palace.

## Where Is India's Sovereignty?

Sovereignty is not a word. It is not a campaign slogan. It is not achieved by renaming cities or putting the Prime Minister's face on government buildings.

Sovereignty is the daily, practical ability to make your own decisions — about your energy, your allies, your workers, your voice in the world, your vote at the United Nations — based on your own values and interests, not someone else's.

By every practical measure, India's sovereignty has been quietly, systematically traded away under this government:

- **Energy sovereignty**: traded for a 30-day American permission slip
- **Labour sovereignty**: traded when Indian workers were dispatched at a foreign PM's personal request to replace a people being bombed
- **Diplomatic sovereignty**: traded when India went silent at the UN on a conflict where justice was clear
- **Strategic sovereignty**: traded when India's Chabahar investment — years of patient diplomacy with Iran — was left undefended as American bombs fell

The house is India's. The mortgage appears to belong to someone else.

## The Workers Deserve Better. So Does India.

The young men queuing in the cold in Lucknow are not villains. They are victims — of unemployment, of debt, of a government that has failed to create the jobs it promised, of a system that has left them with no better option than to work in a war zone.

They deserve jobs at home. They deserve an economy where a 26-year-old carpenter does not have to risk bombs to earn a living wage. They deserve a government that fights for their dignity rather than renting it out to foreign governments as cheap labor.

And India — the nation — deserves a Prime Minister who understands that sovereignty is not a photo opportunity.

Indira Gandhi did not need Netanyahu's approval to run the Indian economy. She did not need Trump's permission to buy oil. She did not send poor Indians to replace bombed-out Palestinians at a foreign PM's personal request.

She ran a country. She knew whose country it was.

The question for every Indian in the next election is simple: **whose country is this?**

If it is ours — then we need someone willing to act like it.
